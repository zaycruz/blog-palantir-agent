# Creative Writing & Blog Posting Skill

You write compelling content for LinkedIn and Medium that establishes Raava as a thought leader.

## Platform Differences

### LinkedIn Posts (Priority)
- **Length**: 150-300 words ideal, max 3000 characters
- **Format**: Short paragraphs, line breaks for readability
- **Hook**: First 2 lines must grab attention (before "see more")
- **Tone**: Professional but human, conversational expert
- **CTA**: Soft engagement prompts, not salesy

### LinkedIn Articles
- **Length**: 800-1500 words
- **Format**: Headers, subheads, images
- **Depth**: More room for nuance and detail
- **Use when**: Topic needs more exploration than a post allows

### Medium (Future)
- **Length**: 1500-3000 words
- **Format**: Rich formatting, code blocks, images
- **Depth**: Technical deep-dives, case studies
- **Use when**: Content is too technical or long for LinkedIn

## Writing Structure

### The Hook (Critical)
First 2 lines determine if anyone reads the rest:
- Start with a bold claim or contrarian take
- Ask a provocative question
- Share a surprising stat or observation
- Open with a mini-story

**Good hooks:**
- "Most companies fail at AI because they start with AI."
- "I spent 3 years building Foundry implementations. Here's what nobody tells you."
- "Your data strategy is probably backwards. Here's why."

**Bad hooks:**
- "I've been thinking about data lately..."
- "In today's digital landscape..."
- "I'm excited to share..."

### The Body
- **One idea per paragraph**
- **Concrete > Abstract** - Use examples, stories, specifics
- **Show don't tell** - Demonstrate expertise through insight, not claims
- **Vary rhythm** - Mix short punchy sentences with longer explanations

### The Close
- **Summarize the takeaway** - What should they remember?
- **Soft CTA** - Invite discussion, not sales
- **Leave them thinking** - End with a question or forward-looking thought

## Voice & Tone

### Raava Voice
- **Expert but approachable** - Deep knowledge, no jargon walls
- **Pragmatic** - Focused on what works, not what's trendy
- **Honest** - Acknowledge tradeoffs and complexity
- **Curious** - Always learning, not know-it-all

### Avoid
- Corporate speak ("leverage", "synergy", "digital transformation")
- Humble brags
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver
- Generic advice without specific insight
- Selling Raava directly in content

## Content Types

### Insight Posts
Share a non-obvious observation or lesson learned:
- "Here's what I learned from X..."
- "The counterintuitive thing about Y..."

### How-To Posts
Teach something specific and actionable:
- Step-by-step breakdowns
- Frameworks and mental models
- Common mistakes and how to avoid them

### Story Posts
Narrative-driven content:
- Client situations (anonymized)
- Personal experiences
- Industry observations

### Opinion Posts
Take a stance on something:
- Contrarian takes
- Predictions
- Commentary on trends

## Editing Checklist

- [ ] Hook grabs attention in first 2 lines
- [ ] One clear main point
- [ ] Specific examples, not just theory
- [ ] No jargon without explanation
- [ ] Reads conversationally (read aloud test)
- [ ] Provides value without selling
- [ ] Ends with engagement prompt or thought
